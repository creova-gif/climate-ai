🛠 Technology Architecture
→
Forecast Layer: ICPAC Masika/Vuli seasonal forecasts (free, public API). FEWS NET food security alerts.
→
Translation Engine: Claude AI (Anthropic) — ICPAC data → plain Swahili advisory in under 3 seconds.
→
Delivery Channels: WhatsApp Business API, Africa's Talking SMS + USSD. Feature-phone compatible.
→
Data Collection: Community Climate Ledger — farmer observations via 30-second USSD/WhatsApp flow.
→
Backend: Supabase (PostgreSQL) — farmer registry, observation data, advisory history.
📝 Problem Statement (Grant-Ready)

Africa's climate forecasting infrastructure has improved dramatically — ICPAC now produces seasonal outlooks 2–3 months ahead with documented 65–72% accuracy for East Africa. But this data does not reach the 65% of Africans who live in rural areas where climate consequences are most severe.

In Tanzania, 65% of the workforce depends on rain-fed agriculture. When ICPAC issues a drought forecast for Dodoma, that information reaches the national meteorological office — and then stops. The 312,000 smallholder farmers in Dodoma who need to change their planting decisions receive nothing.

Hali ya Hewa ("State of the Weather") closes this translation gap — connecting ICPAC's seasonal science to farmers' mobile phones, in Swahili, via WhatsApp and USSD, before each planting season.
